A medication error, according to the National Coordinating Council for Pharmaceutical Error Reporting and Prevention, is any incidence that might have been avoided that led to improper medication usage or patient injury while the patient, healthcare provider or consumer was in possession of the medication (3).

In the practice of anesthesia as well as other hospital settings, medication errors are frequent. Additionally, mistakes could happen at any point during the prescribing process, slips, lapses, or mistakes can result in errors. (4).

Each year, more than 100,000 reports of suspected medication errors are sent to the U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA), examines and arranges the reports to ascertain the nature and origin of the issue; the reports are submitted to the organization's Med Watch safety information and adverse event reporting program by drug manufacturers, medical professionals, and patients. Major negative effects from taking a medication at the wrong dosage might include birth defects, incapacity, hospitalization, and even death (5).
